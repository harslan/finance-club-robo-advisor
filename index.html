AI-Powered Robo-Advisor Project

Finance & Investing Club | Suffolk University Sawyer Business School

What We're Building

An AI-powered robo-advisor that runs parallel to your Tremont Fund decisions. Each week, you'll see three different perspectives on the same investment choices:

  • AI recommendations based on market analysis and financial data
  • Quantitative models using Monte Carlo simulation and portfolio optimization
  • Your actual decisions from the Finance Club's investment process
The Learning Hook: By comparing these three approaches over time, you'll develop sophisticated insights about when to trust AI, when quantitative models matter most, and where human judgment is irreplaceable. This isn't about replacing your decision-making—it's about understanding how AI can augment it.

Three-Phase Development

Phase 1: Foundation

Goal: Build core quantitative tools

  • Monte Carlo simulation for portfolio risk assessment
  • Portfolio optimization using Modern Portfolio Theory
  • Basic asset allocation models
  • Backtesting framework

Quick win: A working tool that suggests portfolio allocations

Phase 2: AI Integration

Goal: Add AI-powered analysis

  • Market sentiment analysis from financial news and earnings calls
  • AI-assisted investment thesis generation
  • Risk factor identification from SEC filings
  • Comparative dashboard showing AI vs. quant recommendations

This is where it gets interesting: seeing AI analyze what you're already researching

Phase 3: Comparative Analysis

Goal: Build systematic insights

  • Track and analyze differences between AI, quant, and human decisions
  • Performance attribution by decision source
  • Case study repository of interesting divergences
  • Best practices guide for AI in investment research

The payoff: Real data on how AI performs vs. human judgment in finance

Technical Approach

Backend Python with libraries for financial analysis (pandas, numpy, scipy) and optimization (cvxpy)
AI Layer Claude API for analyzing financial documents, market sentiment, and generating investment insights
Frontend Streamlit for Phase 1-2 (fast, Python-native), potentially React later if needed
Data Sources Yahoo Finance, SEC EDGAR filings, financial news APIs—all free or have free tiers

No prior coding required: We'll structure this so students with different skill levels can contribute—from Python development to investment analysis to case study documentation.

How This Works with Tremont Fund

The basic idea: AI and quantitative models generate recommendations that you can compare against your actual decisions. Over time, you build a collection of insights about when each approach adds value.

The exact workflow depends on how your club operates—we can figure out what makes sense on Thursday.

What Students Learn

  • AI Literacy: Hands-on experience with LLMs in finance, understanding capabilities and limitations
  • Quantitative Skills: Python programming, Monte Carlo simulation, portfolio optimization
  • Critical Thinking: When to trust AI recommendations vs. human judgment
  • Professional Skills: Building portfolio-worthy projects, collaborative development, technical communication
  • Industry Readiness: Experience with tools and approaches used by modern robo-advisors and quantitative firms
Reality Check: This is ambitious. Not every phase needs to happen, and the scope can adapt based on student interest and bandwidth. The goal is meaningful learning, not building a production system. Even Phase 1 alone would be valuable.

Connection to SAIL Framework

This project embodies Suffolk's SAIL approach to AI education:

Social Intelligence Collaborative decision-making between AI systems and human teams; understanding group dynamics in AI-augmented environments
AI Literacy Practical experience with AI capabilities and limitations; critical evaluation of AI outputs in high-stakes contexts
Innovation/Inquiry Student-led exploration of AI in finance; systematic investigation of human-AI collaboration patterns
Leadership Finance Club leading AI education at SBS; building capabilities that position Suffolk students ahead of the curve

By developing this project, the Finance Club becomes a visible example of how Suffolk students are preparing for AI-augmented careers—creating a showcase for prospective students, industry partners, and the broader business school community.
